What is momentum? ▼
Momentum ($p = mv$) is a vector quantity measuring a body's structural motion threshold. Direction mandates strict utilization of sign assignments ($+$ or $-$).
What is a coalescing collision? ▼
A perfectly inelastic impact condition where structural coupling causes colliding masses to bind completely and move forward with a single unified velocity vector in the arena.
Why is momentum conserved? ▼
Per Newton's Third Law, contact forces during coupling are equal and opposite. Absent net external horizontal friction impulses on the arena floor, total system momentum remains locked.
Why is kinetic energy lost? ▼
Kinetic energy is scalar. The latching process converts available dynamic energy instantly into heat, acoustic vibrations, and structural binding work.
